5 Segmentation Example

5.1 Oregon US-20

This section will demonstrate the segmentation process used for the Oregon US-20 example route. The general route limits are shown in Figure 5.1. The route generally extends from the Jefferson County line to Cline Falls Hwy/OB Riley Rd in Tumalo (44.399010616037145, -121.68160872108665; 44.12954543870768, -121.32386641302361). It is about 25 miles in length.

Figure 5.1: Oregon US-20 Route Boundaries

The first step in the segmenation process was to identify segments based on cross section and passing configuration (for two-lane highways). This is shown in Figure 5.2, with the color coding set as follows.

Based on directional markings; for this example, the southeast-bound (SE-bound) direction of travel:
- Blue: Multilane highway (not present in this route)
- Red: Two-lane highway; passing not permitted in opposing direction
- Yellow: Two-lane highway; can pass using the opposing lane
- Green: Two-lane highway with designated passing lane
- Cyan: Arterial

Figure 5.2: Oregon US-20 Full Route, with segmentation shown for cross section and passing designation

More zoomed images for the north, central, and south portions of the route are shown in Figures 5.3, 5.3, 5.4, and 5.5.

Figure 5.3: Oregon US-20 Segmentation, North Portion of Route

Figure 5.4: Oregon US-20 Segmentation, Central Portion of Route

Figure 5.5: Oregon US-20 Segmentation, South Portion of Route

Locations with horizontal curves along the route are readily seen, but are also explicitly shown in Figure 5.6.

Only horizontal curve 4 meets the segmentation criterion of only explicitly considering horizontal curves with a classification of 3 or higher. This curve has a superelevation of 0, central angle of 31.3 degrees, radius of 716 ft, and a length of 391 ft, yielding a classification of 3.

Figure 5.6: Oregon US-20 Segmentation, including Horizontal Curves

The vertical alignment along this route is quite varying. Consequently, Figure 5.7 only shows the grades that meet the segmentation criterion of vertical class 2 or higher.

Figure 5.7: Oregon US-20 Segmentation, including Significant Grades

The segmentation legend is shown in Figure 5.8.

Figure 5.8: Oregon US-20 Segmentation Legend

Regarding the vertical alignment, the following decisions were made:
- Grades 1 and 2, in segment 3, were similar and thus combined into a single grade, which was of class 3.
- There is a grade that starts in the second half of the segment preceding the second passing lane segment (#27). This grade is not considered in segment 26. The grade levels off in the last fourth of the passing lane segment. The passing lane is not split based upon grade and instead grade #4 is applied to the full length of the passing lane.
